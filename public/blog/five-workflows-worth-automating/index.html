The Five Workflows Worth Automating First

Not every process needs AI. These five workflows tend to have the fastest payoff when you automate them.

On this page

  • 1. Recurring reports assembled by hand
  • 2. Data entry between systems that don’t talk to each other
  • 3. Intake and approval workflows
  • 4. Follow-ups that fall through the cracks
  • 5. Anything with a spreadsheet as the source of truth
  • What doesn’t make this list

“AI automation” gets pitched as a solution to almost everything, which makes it hard to tell what’s actually worth doing first. In practice, a handful of workflow types consistently pay for themselves fast.

1. Recurring reports assembled by hand

If someone on your team spends part of every week pulling numbers from three or four places into one report, that’s almost always automatable — and it’s usually the single highest-ROI automation available.

2. Data entry between systems that don’t talk to each other

Anywhere the same information gets typed twice — once into your CRM, again into your invoicing tool — is time and an error risk you don’t need.

3. Intake and approval workflows

Forms that turn into an email chain, that turn into a decision three days later, are a good candidate for a structured, trackable workflow instead.

4. Follow-ups that fall through the cracks

If leads or client requests sometimes just get missed because nobody circled back, that’s not a people problem — it’s a process that needs a safety net.

5. Anything with a spreadsheet as the source of truth

Spreadsheets are great until three people are editing one at once. If a spreadsheet is quietly running a core part of your operation, it’s worth asking whether it should be.

What doesn’t make this list

Anything that requires real judgment — pricing exceptions, difficult client conversations, hiring decisions. Automation should remove the busywork around those decisions, not make the decision itself.
