I started in telecommunications and network engineering before moving into cybersecurity and compliance work — which means I think about a system the way an attacker, an auditor, and an engineer all would at once.

nehwe.tech is one person, not an agency. That's deliberate: every finding, every line of code, and every automated workflow gets built by the same set of hands that scoped it. No handoffs, no bench of juniors running the actual work while someone senior signs off.

## Why local businesses

Small and mid-sized businesses in Colorado Springs are often stuck between two bad options: hire a full internal team they don't need yet, or hand critical work to a vendor who's never on the phone when something breaks. I work project-by-project or on retainer, and I stay reachable.

## Philosophy

Security, web design, and automation aren't really three separate disciplines — they're three angles on the same question: does this system actually hold up under real conditions? A website that isn't hardened is a security problem. A compliance report nobody can act on is a communication problem. An automation nobody understands after I leave isn't actually finished. I try to build things that hold up on all three counts.
